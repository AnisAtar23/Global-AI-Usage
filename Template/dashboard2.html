Key Findings — Global AI Project: Adoption, Usage & Growth
① Gender Gap in AI Adoption

Male students are 18% more likely to use AI tools daily than female students globally, with the gap narrowing significantly in Northern Europe and North America.

② STEM vs. Humanities

STEM students adopt AI tools 2.3x faster than humanities students, primarily using them for coding assistance, data analysis, and research summarisation.

③ Income & Access Disparity

Students in low-income countries report 3x fewer institutional resources for AI access, highlighting a growing digital equity challenge in global education.

④ AI for Productivity

74% of students report that AI tools save them at least 3 hours per week on assignments, with essay drafting and literature reviews being the top use cases.

⑤ Ethical Concerns on the Rise

61% of educators express concern about academic integrity in the age of AI, yet only 29% of institutions have a formal AI usage policy in place for students.

⑥ Future Outlook

Projections indicate that by 2027, over 90% of university students worldwide will integrate AI tools into their daily academic workflows, reshaping education models globally.
